When the Yankees win, so do local businesses

The game day performance and popularity of the New York Yankees determine whether businesses in the shadow of Yankee Stadium struggle or thrive.

By Dan Whateley

Nov. 25, 2018

Opening day at Yankee Stadium generated $5.5 million in indirect revenue for New York City businesses this year. For local businesses working in the vicinity of the stadium, the Yankees' season generates the largest portion of their annual revenues.

When New York Yankees’ outfielder Aaron Judge won AL Rookie of the Year last year, Bronx businesses near Yankee Stadium saw a bump in sales, said Cary Goodman, executive director at the 161 Street Business Investment District.

He says the Yankees’ 100 wins this year, the team’s best record since its World Series victory in 2009, has been a boon for the local economy. “I think most of our businesses would point to an increase in revenue in souvenirs, sandwiches and suds,” he said.

"If they win the World Series, it's going to be at least two years of good business," said Abdul Abdulla, salesman at Ballpark Sports Shop.

"Sometimes it's scary. If you didn't make anything in the season, that's it.”

Mohammed Abdulla sells Yankees’ caps, jerseys and other official MLB gear at Pinstripe Collectibles, an independently-owned souvenir shop next to Yankee Stadium. He says a poorly attended Yankees’ season can cause financial trouble for the store.

"Sometimes it's scary,” Abdulla said, standing in front of rows of blue and white Yankees’ caps in the River Avenue shop. "If you didn't make anything in the season, that's it.”

Abdulla said Pinstripe Collectibles had to apply for a bank loan to cover losses in 2016. Average Yankees’ home game attendance that year dropped to a 10-year low of 37,819 as the team’s record fell to 84-78.

In 2017, home game attendance rose as the team improved to a record of 91-71. "We stayed alive," Abdulla said.

"If they're doing good, we're doing good,” he said. “If they're doing bad, we're doing bad."
